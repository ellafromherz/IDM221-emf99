Design I for Media Projects

Expressive Line Project

The final template of my Expressive Line Project for my class, Design I for Media. I focused on manipulating line weight and type to create a dynamic abstract design. I abstracted my initials to create dominant foreground designs, and used line repetitions (inspired by photos) to create subordinate background patterns. I worked in two stages: formal sketches and final outcome. In addition to showing different types of design, the thick-weight foreground design express the mood happy. The final outcome consisted of both a hand drawn/inked layout of refined designs and a final digital layout of the best possible digital combinations of my foreground and background designs.

Shape and Pattern Project

The final template of my Shape and Pattern Project for my class, Design I for Media. In this project, I explored the tension of equivocal balance between black + white and positive + negative shapes. I also explored ideas of balance through symmetry and asymmetry and using complex shapes to create a pattern with an ambiguous figure ground relationship. The project had three components: a hand-cut paper Notan, digital patterns with crops derived from my Notan, and a final concept design - making a digital composite of my pattern into a proposed space.

Value Translation Project

The final template of my Value Translation Project for my class, Design I for Media. I created a self-portrait that is inspired by a film-still of a portrait that I sourced from an assigned movie. Once I chose my inspiration and using my own means - a camera, lighting, props, and mimicry, I did the best I could to recreate the pose, value, lighting, and expression in your selection. Next, I digitally broke down the value pattern in black, white, and shades of gray in my image to fill into a grid. I digitally added a color-coded system based on colors+ numbers that fills in the grid based on translating the values of my image into a coded map. This Map assisted me in identifying the value pattern. Next, I created a value scale through adjusting images of different textures, along with nine different sketches that represented the photographed textures. Finally, I used the posterized portrait and inserted my textures and sketches to create a portrait.

Perspective Studies Project

The final template of my Persoective Studies Project for my class, Design I for Media. I began with painting a 9-step value scale using black and white acrylic paint. I followed a series of instructions and tips to create a consistent grayscale with even gradations between steps. Next, in Adobe Illustrator, I created digital designs keeping space in mind. In pen and ink, I created a 1-point perspective study; and also created a 2-point perspective study that I painted with the colors that correlate with the original value scale.
